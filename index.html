RESEARCH PROJECT 1

Alcohol exposure in a developing brain negatively impacts proper cortical development. In a mouse brain, we have seen that alcohol leads to aberrant migration of cortical GABAergic interneurons. Through perforated patch clamp recording, immunohistochemistry, and Barnez Maze behavioral test, I found that I can pharmacologically prevent the effect of alcohol by using bumetanide, NKCC1 chloride importer inhibitor.

RESEARCH PROJECT 2

While bumetanide treatment prevents the effect of alcohol on aberrant cortical development, bumetanide is a loop diuretic and thus, difficult to prescribe to pregnant women. As another potential drug treatment, I investigated if nifedipine, which blocks L-type voltage-gated calcium channel, can prevent the effect of alcohol as a downstream mechanism of NKCC1. With whole-cell patch clamp recording and live imaging embryonic neurons, I found to date that nifedipine noramlized the effect of alcohol in the developing mouse cortex.
